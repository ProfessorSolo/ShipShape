## What we've covered

We laid the foundational philosophy needed before we start wiring up tools. By the end of this lesson, we have covered:

- DevOps as an approach to improving how software is **built, shipped, run, and improved**
- the old Dev vs Ops split and the friction between **change** and **stability**
- **CI/CD** and repeatable pipeline thinking
- **Infrastructure as Code** as a move away from memory-based setup
- **immutable infrastructure** as a way to reduce fragile manual patching
- **observability** as a way to understand running systems after deployment
- how these ideas apply even to a **simple Node app**, not just giant enterprise systems

## Bearing for the next leg

The main point of this lesson is this:

<NoteBox title="The Real Finish Line">
  **Software is not done when the code works on one machine.** Professional software needs a delivery story, an environment story, and an operations story.
</NoteBox>

That is the course we are setting before we start packaging the application.

## Suggested Discussion Prompts

1. Why is “it works on my machine” not an acceptable finish line?
2. What kinds of problems happen when setup lives only in someone’s memory?
3. Why might operations teams resist constant change?
4. Why is a repeatable build/deploy process more valuable than a clever undocumented one?
5. Why do logs matter after deployment?
